<h1>Colors</h1>
<h2>Steps 1-2: Backgrounds</h2>
<p>Steps 1 and 2 are designed for app backgrounds and subtle component backgrounds. You can use them interchangeably, depending on the vibe you're going for.</p>

<p>Appropriate applications include:</p>
<ul>
<li>Main app background</li>
<li>Striped table background</li>
<li>Code block background</li>
<li>Card background</li>
<li>Sidebar background</li>
<li>Canvas area background</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps 3–5: Component backgrounds</h2>

<p>Steps 3, 4, and 5 are designed for UI component backgrounds.</p>
<ul>
<li>Step 3 is for normal states.</li>
<li>Step 4 is for hover states.</li>
<li>Step 5 is for pressed or selected states.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps 6-8: Borders</h2>

<p>Steps 6, 7, and 8 are designed for borders.</p>
<ul>
<li>Step 6 is designed for subtle borders on components which are not interactive. For example sidebars, headers, cards, alerts, and separators.</li>
<li>Step 7 is designed for subtle borders on interactive components.</li>
<li>Step 8 is designed for stronger borders on interactive components and focus rings.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Steps 9-10: Solid backgrounds</h2>

<p>Step 9 has the highest chroma of all steps in the scale. In other words, it's the purest step, the step mixed with the least amount of white or black. Because 9 is the purest step, it has a wide range of applications:</p>
<ul>
<li>Website/App backgrounds</li>
<li>Website section backgrounds</li>
<li>Header backgrounds</li>
<li>Component backgrounds</li>
<li>Graphics/Logos</li>
<li>Overlays</li>
<li>Coloured shadows</li>
<li>Accent borders</li>
</ul>

<p>Step 10 is designed for component hover states, where step 9 is the component's normal state background.</p>

<h2>Steps 11-12: Text</h2>
<p>Steps 11 and 12 are designed for text.</p>
<ul>
<li>Step 11 is designed for low-contrast text.</li>
<li>Step 12 is designed for high-contrast text.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Custom brand colors</h3>
<p>Radix Colors are not intended to be customised. They’re designed to be accessible, well-balanced, and harmonious. Any customisation would likely break these features.</p>
<p>If you need custom brand colors, we recommend adding custom scales alongside Radix scales.</p>
<p>For example, you could use Radix Colors for your gray scale and your semantic scales, then add custom brand/accent colors.</p>

<h2>Choosing a gray scale</h2>
<ul>
<li> <strong>gray </strong>is pure gray</li>
<li><strong>mauve</strong> is based on a purple hue</li>
<li><strong>slate</strong> is based on a blue hue</li>
<li><strong>sage </strong>is based on a green hue</li>
<li><strong>olive</strong> is based on a lime hue</li>
<li><strong>sand </strong>is based on a yellow hue</li>
</ul>

<h3>Neutral pairing</h3>
<p>If you want a neutral vibe, or you want to keep things simple, gray will work well with any hue or palette.</p>

<h3>Natural pairing</h3>
<p>Alternatively, choose the gray scale which is saturated with the hue closest to your accent hue. The difference is subtle, but this will create a more colorful and harmonius vibe.</p>
